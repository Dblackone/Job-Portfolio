## Before and After Story
Justice Omoyele Residential Duplex in Ado-Ekiti began as a construction consultancy engagement: design concept development, site supervision, and construction consultancy during the structural build. The project did not end at handover of the shell. We transitioned directly into the interior design phase — kitchen setup, bedroom fitting, TV wall installations, and functional space integration — completing the full residential scope from structural oversight to occupied home.

---

## Project Results
A completed duplex in Ado-Ekiti: structure built to specification, interior designed and delivered to the client's brief. Every room resolved — kitchen operational, bedrooms fitted, TV wall installed, and living spaces integrated and furnished. The client received a finished home, not a finished building.

---

## Client Value
End-to-end delivery: the client engaged one team for both the construction consultancy and the interior design. No coordination gap between the contractor's scope and the interior consultant's requirements. Interior specifications were set during construction, which meant finishes, slab heights, and wall positions were informed by the interior brief before concrete was poured.

---

## Site Lesson
Continuity between construction and interior phases is not a convenience — it is a quality decision. When the same team holds both scopes, design intent does not get lost at the handover boundary.
